Rolling ☆ Girls Review Episode 1 Review (Spoilers)

Otaku Reaction by Episode
0
1:31 AM January 13, 2015
HoneyFeed

Summary

If you're looking for something fun and exciting with an adventurous twist and refreshing world-view, you are here for treat with "Rolling Girls." Somehow unrelated to what the title suggests--we see a lot of similar titles with girls in jazz bands or playing rock music--this anime has little to do with music (although still strictly related to girls). Most, if not all, main characters (probably even major supporting characters) are female. We see girls/women in (bare-hand) combats, girls on bikes, girls as fighters, girls as mayors, and girls as leaders (if you like the combat scenes in Bento!, you would definitely want to tag along with this anime).

The story is set in a parallel universe in Japan (there are so many parallel universes these days...), 10 years after the "Great Tokyo War." After the great war, regional leaders, directors, and the wealthy miraculously disappeared. The remaining ten prefectures were divided and restructured, and eventually declared independence from the central government. Strong localism grew as ways for the new formed States to distinguish themselves from one another and constant conflicts occur between and among nations as States seek to expand their territories and self-interests. There are two kinds of people in this world: those with special superpowers--"Mosa," and ordinary people--"Mob." Fights usually happen between individual Mosas, and the first fight in this season is between the mercenary president of "East Hill village," Kuniko Shigyou, and the "Peace Contractor" from "Tokorozawa," Machya Green.

Despite the political conflict--East Hill Village is determined to take Tokorozawa in since they don't want to be seen as a rural county--Kuniko is more interested in the true identity hidden under Machya Green's mask, and declares that she would one day add the Machya power suite into her collection. The fight ended with Tokorozawa beating East Hill Village with a three-story tall robot.

Our heroine of the story, however, is not Machya Green, but a girl named Nozomi Moritomo. Nozomi's family owns a Dango (a Japanese snack/street food) shop in town and she recently joined the Niyoshi Town Self-Defence Team, "Propellers," as an intern. The president of Niyoshi Town Self-Defense Team is Masami Utoku, who is really close to Nozomi, as a sister-like figure, and also a suspected candidate for the true identity of Machya Green (according to Kuniko's speculations).

One day when Nozomi is on duty (as an intern) at the Self-Defense Team, a girl with short blue hair, Yukina Kosaka, crashes her bike into the front door to the defense team head quarters (which is just a small office). Turns out that she is looking exactly for the Niyoshi Town Self-Defense Team and we probably will know more about her in the next episode.

After they resolved the bike-crash crisis, a (suspicious) bus waits at the front door and takes the whole team to a theme park. While the team rides excitedly on the roller-coaster, Kuniko appears and a part of the track collapses at a snap of her fingers. This happens to be a clandestine plan of Kuniko's to lure Machya Green out.

Will Machya Green be able to save the team (of course she will, this is not a thriller) and who is the girl in the mask (who secretly puts a note in Machya Green's bike side-bag)? Hopefully we will find out next week~!
